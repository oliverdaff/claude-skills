# Experiment Types

## does-anyone-care

Test whether anyone recognizes the problem as real and painful. Post in a community, DM potential users, or share a problem statement and measure engagement. Example: post "How do you currently handle X?" in a relevant Slack/Discord and track replies, DMs, and emotional intensity.

## will-they-engage

Test whether people will spend time on a solution (not just agree the problem exists). Share a landing page, waitlist, or prototype walkthrough and measure active engagement. Example: create a Loom walkthrough of a mock solution and track watch-through rate and unprompted follow-up questions.

## will-they-commit

Test whether people will put skin in the game — money, time, reputation. Ask for a pre-order, deposit, or public commitment. Example: offer early access for $20 and measure conversion rate from interested to paid.

## fake-product

Present the product as if it exists (without building it) and measure real purchase or signup intent. Example: run a landing page with a "Buy Now" button that leads to a "coming soon" page, and measure click-through rate.

## concierge

Deliver the value proposition manually to a small number of users. You are the product. Example: manually curate and send weekly reports that the product would automate, then measure retention and willingness to pay after 3 weeks.

## behaviour

Observe what people actually do (not what they say). Instrument existing tools, shadow workflows, or analyse logs. Example: watch 5 users do the task your product would replace and time each step, noting workarounds and frustration points.
